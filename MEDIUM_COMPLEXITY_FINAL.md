# MEDIUM Complexity Words - Final List

Words from the 1000 most common that need notes to avoid confusion, but don't require learning entire grammar principles.

**Note:** Articles (el, la, un, una, los, las) and standalone pronouns (me, te, le, se, nos, os) are excluded from the database as standalone words, so they're not included here.

## 1. ACCENT MARKS CHANGE MEANING (CRITICAL - MEDIUM)

These are the most confusing because they look identical but mean different things:

- **qué** (rank 13) vs **que** (rank 1)
  - qué = "what" (question word)
  - que = "that, which" (relative pronoun)
  - Note needed: "¿Qué es esto?" (What is this?) vs "El libro que leí" (The book that I read)

- **cuándo** (rank 428) vs **cuando** (rank 52)
  - cuándo = "when" (question)
  - cuando = "when" (conjunction/time)
  - Note needed: "¿Cuándo vienes?" (When are you coming?) vs "Cuando llegué" (When I arrived)

- **dónde** (rank 90) vs **donde** (rank 152)
  - dónde = "where" (question)
  - donde = "where" (relative)
  - Note needed: "¿Dónde está?" (Where is it?) vs "La casa donde vivo" (The house where I live)

- **cuál** (rank 347) vs **cual** (rank 539)
  - cuál = "which" (question)
  - cual = "which" (relative, formal)
  - Note needed: "¿Cuál prefieres?" (Which do you prefer?) vs "El cual es mejor" (Which one is better)

- **cuánto** (rank 327) vs **cuanto** (rank 534)
  - cuánto = "how much" (question)
  - cuanto = "as much as" (comparison)
  - Note needed: "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (How much does it cost?) vs "Tanto cuanto puedas" (As much as you can)

- **cuántos** (rank 954) - "how many" (question)

- **adónde** (rank 532) - "where to" (question with movement)
  - Note needed: "¿Adónde vas?" (Where are you going to?)

- **porqué** (rank 912) vs **porque** (rank 86)
  - porqué = "why" (noun: "the reason why")
  - porque = "because" (conjunction)
  - Note needed: "No sé el porqué" (I don't know the reason why) vs "Vine porque quería" (I came because I wanted to)

- **sí** (rank 29) vs **si** (rank 23)
  - sí = "yes"
  - si = "if"
  - Note needed: CRITICAL! "Sí, quiero" (Yes, I want) vs "Si quieres, vamos" (If you want, let's go)

- **más** (rank 37) vs **mas** (rank 233)
  - más = "more"
  - mas = "but" (formal, literary - rare but exists)
  - Note needed: "Más grande" (Bigger) vs "Mas no pudo" (But he couldn't - formal)

- **cómo** (rank 59) vs **como** (rank 35)
  - cómo = "how" (question)
  - como = "like, as" (comparison)
  - Note needed: "¿Cómo estás?" (How are you?) vs "Como tú" (Like you)

## 2. TWO "TO BE" VERBS (MEDIUM)

- **ser** (rank 69) vs **estar** (rank 136)
  - ser = permanent characteristics, identity, time, origin, profession
  - estar = temporary states, location, emotions, conditions
  - Note needed: "Soy médico" (I am a doctor - permanent) vs "Estoy cansado" (I am tired - temporary)
  - Note needed: "La comida es buena" (The food is good - inherent quality) vs "La comida está buena" (The food tastes good right now)

## 3. TWO "FOR" PREPOSITIONS (MEDIUM)

- **por** (rank 12) vs **para** (rank 20)
  - por = reason, duration, exchange, "through", "by means of", "for the sake of"
  - para = purpose, destination, deadline, "in order to", "intended for"
  - Note needed: "Lo hice por ti" (I did it for you - reason) vs "Es para ti" (It's for you - destination/purpose)
  - Note needed: "Por la mañana" (During the morning) vs "Para mañana" (By/for tomorrow - deadline)

## 4. CONTRACTIONS - ONLY 2 IN SPANISH! (MEDIUM)

- **del** (rank 33) - "of the" (de + el)
  - Note needed: This is one of only TWO contractions in Spanish! "La casa del profesor" (The professor's house)

- **al** (rank 34) - "to the" (a + el)
  - Note needed: This is one of only TWO contractions in Spanish! "Voy al banco" (I'm going to the bank)

## 5. "THERE IS/ARE" - MULTIPLE FORMS (MEDIUM)

- **hay** (rank 44) - "there is/are" (present)
- **había** (rank 202) - "there was/were" (past)
- **habrá** (rank 612) - "there will be" (future)
- **habría** (rank 655) - "there would be" (conditional)
- Note needed: "Hay un problema" (There is a problem - present) vs "Había un problema" (There was a problem - past)

## 6. TIME EXPRESSION "AGO" (MEDIUM)

- **hace** (rank 107) - "ago" in time expressions
  - Note needed: "Hace dos días" = "two days ago" (backwards from English!)
  - Also means "does, makes" - context-dependent: "Hace calor" (It's hot - makes heat)

## 7. DEMONSTRATIVES - GENDER & DISTANCE (MEDIUM)

- **este** (rank 54) - "this (masculine, near)"
- **esta** (rank 41) - "this (feminine, near)"
- **esto** (rank 38) - "this (neuter, unknown noun)"
- **ese** (rank 80) - "that (masculine, medium distance)"
- **esa** (rank 94) - "that (feminine, medium distance)"
- **eso** (rank 27) - "that (neuter, unknown noun)"
- Note needed: Spanish has 3 distances (este/ese/aquel) vs English 2 (this/that)
- Note needed: esto/eso used when noun is unknown: "¿Qué es esto?" (What is this?) not "¿Qué es este?"

## 8. POSSESSIVES - AGREEMENT & AMBIGUITY (MEDIUM)

- **mi** (rank 21) vs **mis** (rank 134) - "my" (singular vs plural)
  - Note needed: "Mi casa" (my house) vs "Mis casas" (my houses)

- **tu** (rank 31) vs **tus** (rank 172) - "your" (singular vs plural)
  - Note needed: "Tu libro" (your book) vs "Tus libros" (your books)

- **su** (rank 30) vs **sus** (rank 85) - "his/her/your/their" (AMBIGUOUS!)
  - Note needed: "Su casa" could mean "his house", "her house", "your house", or "their house"! Context determines meaning.

## 9. QUESTION VS RELATIVE PRONOUNS (MEDIUM)

- **quién** (rank 88) vs **quien** (rank 212)
  - quién = "who" (question)
  - quien = "who" (relative pronoun)
  - Note needed: "¿Quién es?" (Who is it?) vs "La persona quien vino" (The person who came)

## 10. PERSONAL "A" (MEDIUM)

- **a** (rank 4) - "to, at"
  - Note needed: Personal "a" rule - use "a" before direct object if it's a person
  - "Veo a María" (I see María) vs "Veo el libro" (I see the book)
  - This is unique to Spanish! No equivalent in English.

## 11. "DE" MULTIPLE USES (MEDIUM)

- **de** (rank 2) - "of, from"
  - Possession: "El libro de Juan" (Juan's book)
  - Origin: "Soy de México" (I'm from Mexico)
  - Material: "Casa de madera" (Wooden house)
  - Time: "De día" (During the day)
  - Note needed: Very versatile preposition with many uses

## 12. SPECIAL VERB CONSTRUCTIONS (MEDIUM)

- **gusta** (rank 194) / **gustan** (rank 838) - "like" but backwards!
  - Note needed: "Me gusta el café" = "Coffee pleases me" = "I like coffee"
  - Subject and object are reversed from English
  - "Me gusta" (singular) vs "Me gustan" (plural): "Me gusta el libro" vs "Me gustan los libros"

- **tener que** (rank 189 + que) - "have to" (obligation)
  - Note needed: "Tengo que ir" = "I have to go" (obligation)
  - Different from "tener" alone = "to have/possess": "Tengo un libro" (I have a book)

## 13. TIME PREPOSITIONS (MEDIUM)

- **desde** (rank 176) - "since, from" (time origin)
  - Note needed: "Desde ayer" (since yesterday) vs "Desde las 5" (since 5 o'clock)

- **hasta** (rank 118) - "until, even" (time endpoint)
  - Note needed: "Hasta mañana" (until tomorrow) vs "Hasta las 10" (until 10 o'clock)
  - Also means "even": "Hasta yo lo sé" (Even I know it)

- **durante** (rank 397) - "during" (duration)
  - Note needed: "Durante el día" (during the day)

## 14. "CON" SPECIAL FORMS (MEDIUM)

- **con** (rank 19) - "with"
- **conmigo** (rank 209) - "with me" (special form)
- **contigo** (rank 225) - "with you" (special form)
- Note needed: "Conmigo" not "con yo", "contigo" not "con tú"
- "Ven conmigo" (Come with me) vs "Voy con él" (I go with him - regular form)

## 15. "TIEMPO" DUAL MEANING (MEDIUM)

- **tiempo** (rank 98) - "time, weather"
  - Note needed: "No tengo tiempo" (I don't have time) vs "¿Qué tiempo hace?" (What's the weather like?)

## 16. "MAÑANA" DUAL MEANING (MEDIUM)

- **mañana** (rank 180) - "morning, tomorrow"
  - Note needed: "Por la mañana" (in the morning) vs "Mañana voy" (tomorrow I go)

## Summary: MEDIUM Complexity Words in Database

2. **Two "to be"** (2): ser, estar
3. **Two "for"** (2): por, para
4. **Contractions** (2): del, al
5. **"There is" forms** (4): hay, había, habrá, habría
6. **Time "ago"** (1): hace
7. **Demonstratives** (6): este/esta/esto, ese/esa/eso
8. **Possessives** (6): mi/mis, tu/tus, su/sus
9. **Question vs relative** (2 pairs): quién/quien, qué/que, cuándo/cuando, dónde/donde
10. **Personal "a"** (1): a
11. **"De" multiple uses** (1): de
12. **Special constructions** (2): gusta/gustan, tener que
13. **Time prepositions** (3): desde, hasta, durante
14. **"Con" special forms** (3): con, conmigo, contigo
15. **Dual meanings** (2): tiempo, mañana

These words confuse English learners because they:
- Look the same but mean different things (accents)
- Have multiple meanings depending on context
- Work differently than English equivalents
- Have special rules that aren't obvious
