# MEDIUM Complexity Words Analysis

Words from the 1000 most common that need notes to avoid confusion, but don't require learning entire grammar principles.

## 1. Accent Marks Change Meaning (CRITICAL - MEDIUM)

These pairs look identical but have different meanings based on accent:

- **qué** (rank 13) vs **que** (rank 1)
  - qué = "what" (question word)
  - que = "that, which" (relative pronoun)
  - Note: "¿Qué es esto?" vs "El libro que leí"

- **cuándo** (rank 428) vs **cuando** (rank 52)
  - cuándo = "when" (question)
  - cuando = "when" (conjunction/time)
  - Note: "¿Cuándo vienes?" vs "Cuando llegué, estaba cerrado"

- **dónde** (rank 90) vs **donde** (rank 152)
  - dónde = "where" (question)
  - donde = "where" (relative)
  - Note: "¿Dónde está?" vs "La casa donde vivo"

- **cuál** (rank 347) vs **cual** (rank 539)
  - cuál = "which" (question)
  - cual = "which" (relative, formal)
  - Note: "¿Cuál prefieres?" vs "El cual es mejor"

- **cuánto** (rank 327) vs **cuanto** (rank 534)
  - cuánto = "how much" (question)
  - cuanto = "as much as" (comparison)
  - Note: "¿Cuánto cuesta?" vs "Tanto cuanto puedas"

- **cuántos** (rank 954) - "how many" (question)

- **adónde** (rank 532) - "where to" (question with movement)

- **porqué** (rank 912) vs **porque** (rank 86)
  - porqué = "why" (noun: "the reason why")
  - porque = "because" (conjunction)
  - Note: "No sé el porqué" vs "Vine porque quería"

- **sí** (rank 29, 74) vs **si** (rank 23)
  - sí = "yes"
  - si = "if"
  - Note: Critical distinction! "Sí, quiero" vs "Si quieres, vamos"

- **más** (rank 37) vs **mas** (rank 233)
  - más = "more"
  - mas = "but" (formal, literary)
  - Note: "Más grande" vs "Mas no pudo" (rare but exists)

## 2. Two "To Be" Verbs (MEDIUM)

- **ser** (rank 69) vs **estar** (rank 136)
  - ser = permanent characteristics, identity, time, origin
  - estar = temporary states, location, emotions
  - Note: "Soy médico" (I am a doctor) vs "Estoy cansado" (I am tired)
  - Note: "La comida es buena" (inherent quality) vs "La comida está buena" (tastes good right now)

## 3. Two "For" Prepositions (MEDIUM)

- **por** (rank 12) vs **para** (rank 20)
  - por = reason, duration, exchange, "through", "by means of"
  - para = purpose, destination, deadline, "in order to"
  - Note: "Lo hice por ti" (I did it for you - reason) vs "Es para ti" (It's for you - destination)
  - Note: "Por la mañana" (during) vs "Para mañana" (by/for tomorrow)

## 4. Contractions (MEDIUM - Only 2 in Spanish!)

- **del** (rank 33) - "of the" (de + el)
  - Note: Only contraction in Spanish! "La casa del profesor"

- **al** (rank 34) - "to the" (a + el)
  - Note: Only contraction in Spanish! "Voy al banco"

## 5. "There Is/Are" - Multiple Forms (MEDIUM)

- **hay** (rank 44) - "there is/are" (present)
- **había** (rank 202) - "there was/were" (past)
- **habrá** (rank 612) - "there will be" (future)
- **habría** (rank 655) - "there would be" (conditional)
- Note: "Hay un problema" (present) vs "Había un problema" (past)

## 6. Time Expression "Ago" (MEDIUM)

- **hace** (rank 107) - "ago" in time expressions
  - Note: "Hace dos días" = "two days ago" (backwards from English!)
  - Also means "does, makes" - context-dependent

## 7. Demonstratives - Gender & Distance (MEDIUM)

- **este** (rank 54) - "this (masculine, near)"
- **esta** (rank 41) - "this (feminine, near)"
- **esto** (rank 38) - "this (neuter, unknown noun)"
- **ese** (rank 80) - "that (masculine, medium distance)"
- **esa** (rank 94) - "that (feminine, medium distance)"
- **eso** (rank 27) - "that (neuter, unknown noun)"
- **aquel/aquella** - "that (far)" - three-way distinction
- Note: Spanish has 3 distances (este/ese/aquel) vs English 2 (this/that)
- Note: esto/eso used when noun is unknown: "¿Qué es esto?" not "¿Qué es este?"

## 8. Possessives - Agreement & Ambiguity (MEDIUM)

- **mi** (rank 21) vs **mis** (rank 134) - "my" (singular vs plural)
- **tu** (rank 31) vs **tus** (rank 172) - "your" (singular vs plural)
- **su** (rank 30) vs **sus** (rank 85) - "his/her/your/their" (ambiguous!)
  - Note: "Su casa" could mean "his house", "her house", "your house", or "their house"!
  - Context determines meaning

## 9. Question vs Relative Pronouns (MEDIUM)

- **quién** (rank 88) vs **quien** (rank 212)
  - quién = "who" (question)
  - quien = "who" (relative pronoun)
  - Note: "¿Quién es?" vs "La persona quien vino"

## 10. Personal "A" (MEDIUM)

- **a** (rank 4) - "to, at"
  - Note: Personal "a" rule - use "a" before direct object if it's a person
  - "Veo a María" (I see María) vs "Veo el libro" (I see the book)
  - This is unique to Spanish!

## 11. "De" Multiple Uses (MEDIUM)

- **de** (rank 2) - "of, from"
  - Possession: "El libro de Juan"
  - Origin: "Soy de México"
  - Material: "Casa de madera"
  - Time: "De día" (during the day)
  - Note: Very versatile preposition

## 12. Special Verb Constructions (MEDIUM)

- **gusta** (rank 194) / **gustan** (rank 838) - "like" but backwards!
  - Note: "Me gusta el café" = "Coffee pleases me" = "I like coffee"
  - Subject and object are reversed from English
  - "Me gusta" (singular) vs "Me gustan" (plural)

- **hace falta** - "need" but different construction
  - "Me hace falta dinero" = "I need money" (literally: "money is lacking to me")

- **tener que** (rank 189 + que) - "have to" (obligation)
  - "Tengo que ir" = "I have to go"
  - Different from "tener" alone = "to have/possess"

## 13. Time Prepositions (MEDIUM)

- **desde** (rank 176) - "since, from" (time origin)
  - "Desde ayer" = "since yesterday"
  - "Desde las 5" = "since 5 o'clock"

- **hasta** (rank 118) - "until, even" (time endpoint)
  - "Hasta mañana" = "until tomorrow"
  - "Hasta las 10" = "until 10 o'clock"

- **durante** (rank 397) - "during" (duration)
  - "Durante el día" = "during the day"

## 14. "Con" Special Forms (MEDIUM)

- **con** (rank 19) - "with"
- **conmigo** (rank 209) - "with me" (special form)
- **contigo** (rank 225) - "with you" (special form)
- Note: "Conmigo" not "con yo", "contigo" not "con tú"

## 15. "Como" Multiple Meanings (MEDIUM)

- **como** (rank 35) - "like, as, how"
  - Comparison: "Como tú" = "like you"
  - Manner: "Como quieras" = "however you want"
  - Also: "¿Cómo estás?" = "How are you?" (with accent: **cómo** rank 59)

## Summary: MEDIUM Complexity Words

**Total: ~40-50 words** that need notes:

1. **Accent pairs** (9 pairs): qué/que, cuándo/cuando, dónde/donde, cuál/cual, cuánto/cuanto, sí/si, más/mas, porqué/porque, cómo/como
2. **Two "to be"** (2): ser, estar
3. **Two "for"** (2): por, para
4. **Contractions** (2): del, al
5. **"There is" forms** (4): hay, había, habrá, habría
6. **Time "ago"** (1): hace
7. **Demonstratives** (6): este/esta/esto, ese/esa/eso
8. **Possessives** (6): mi/mis, tu/tus, su/sus
9. **Question vs relative** (3 pairs): quién/quien, qué/que, cuándo/cuando, dónde/donde
10. **Personal "a"** (1): a
11. **"De" multiple uses** (1): de
12. **Special constructions** (3): gusta/gustan, tener que, hace falta
13. **Time prepositions** (3): desde, hasta, durante
14. **"Con" special forms** (3): con, conmigo, contigo

These are the words that confuse English learners because they:
- Look the same but mean different things (accents)
- Have multiple meanings depending on context
- Work differently than English equivalents
- Have special rules that aren't obvious

2. **Two "to be"** (2): ser, estar
3. **Two "for"** (2): por, para
4. **Contractions** (2): del, al
5. **"There is" forms** (4): hay, había, habrá, habría
6. **Time "ago"** (1): hace
7. **Demonstratives** (6): este/esta/esto, ese/esa/eso
8. **Possessives** (6): mi/mis, tu/tus, su/sus
9. **Question vs relative** (3 pairs): quién/quien, qué/que, cuándo/cuando, dónde/donde
10. **Personal "a"** (1): a
11. **"De" multiple uses** (1): de
12. **Special constructions** (3): gusta/gustan, tener que, hace falta
13. **Time prepositions** (3): desde, hasta, durante
14. **"Con" special forms** (3): con, conmigo, contigo

These are the words that confuse English learners because they:
- Look the same but mean different things (accents)
- Have multiple meanings depending on context
- Work differently than English equivalents
- Have special rules that aren't obvious
